# Expedia Group — Market Expansion Strategy

A strategic management project completed at Hult International Business School, analyzing Expedia Group's business model and developing recommendations for expanding into emerging markets.

## Project Overview

This was a team project for my Strategic Management course at Hult. We analyzed Expedia's competitive position in the online travel agency (OTA) space and developed actionable recommendations for market expansion, with a focus on the Southeast Asian market.

## My Contribution

- Conducted competitive analysis across Expedia, Booking.com, Airbnb, and regional OTA players
- Researched cultural factors, payment preferences, and travel behaviors in Southeast Asian markets
- Developed localization recommendations including mobile-first design, local payment integration, and regional hotel partnerships
- Created financial projections for market entry including customer acquisition costs and expected revenue

## Key Insights

- Southeast Asia's high mobile usage (80%+ of travel bookings via mobile in key markets) demands a mobile-first approach, not a mobile-adapted one
- Local payment methods (GrabPay, GCash, bank transfers) are non-negotiable for market entry — credit card penetration is too low
- Regional competitors have deeper local hotel partnerships, which Expedia would need to match or surpass
- Brand awareness varies significantly across markets — Indonesia and Thailand need different acquisition strategies than Vietnam or Philippines
